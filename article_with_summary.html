About Paneer Ki Sabji

An easy and simple, yet premium curry recipe prepared with diced paneer cubes in a spicy and tangy onion tomato gravy. Paneer Ki Sabji or Quick Paneer Curry can be an ideal gravy curry that can be served with multiple types of Indian flat breads like roti and chapati but not limited to. It can also be a fancy side dish for flavored rice recipes like jeera rice, pulao, and fried rice.

There are different types of paneer ki sabzi across India, but the most popular way is dhaba-style curry. It is mainly prepared with simple onion and tomato gravy with additional thick yogurt or curd. Adding yogurt makes it thick and creamy and also helps to get that thick gravy consistency. In addition, adding cashew paste, and cooking cream makes it more fancy and more flavored.

How to Make Paneer ki Sabji with Step-by-step Photos

  4. do not overcook the paneer as it turns rubbery. keep aside.

  6. add 1 tsp cumin, 1 bay leaf, 1 pod black cardamom, 1 inch cinnamon, 2 pods cardamom and 4 cloves.
  7. saute on low flame until the spices turn aromatic.
  8. now add 1 onion, 1 tsp ginger garlic paste and saute until the onions turn golden brown.
  9. further keeping the flame on low, add ¼ tsp turmeric, 1 tsp chilli powder, 1 tsp coriander powder, ½ tsp cumin powder, ½ tsp garam masala and 1 tsp salt.
  10. saute on low flame until the spices turn aromatic.
  11. add 2 cup tomato puree and mix well.
  12. cover and cook for 10 minutes or until the oil separates.
  13. keeping the flame on low, add ½ cup curd and stir continuously.
  14. keep cooking until the curd is well combined.
  15. now add 1 cup water and mix well adjusting the consistency as required.

Notes
